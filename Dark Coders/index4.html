ANEMIA

Anemia is a condition in which you lack enough healthy red blood cells to carry adequate oxygen to your body's tissues. Having anemia, also referred to as low hemoglobin, can make you feel tired and weak. There are many forms of anemia, each with its own cause.

What to eat...

Leafy greens-

Leafy greens, especially
dark ones, are
among the best
sources of nonheme iron.

Nuts and seeds-

Many types of nuts
and seeds are good
sources of iron.
They taste great on
their own or sprinkled
on salads or yogurt.

Eggs-

Eggs are known for
their proteins,
but they also pack
a high level of iron.

Fortified Food

There are different
types of food
that are fortified
with iron.

Beans and Pulses

Lentils are supposed
to be a superfood
for anaemic patients.

What not to eat...

Tea and Coffe-

Iron deficiency is the
leading cause of anaemia
and caffeine can
indeed inhibit the
absorption of iron.

Milk or
dairy products –

Cow's milk actually
makes it harder for
the body to absorb iron.

grapes –
Tannins

Tannins and oxalates
are some of the naturally
occurring tea compounds that
are said to inhibit the
absorption of iron.

brown rice

Food like brown rice
impairs the absorption
of iron, zinc and
calcium and may promote
mineral deficiencies.

peanuts, parsley,
and chocolate

Food such as peanuts,
parsley, and chocolate
blocks absorption of iron.

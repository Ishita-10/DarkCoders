THYROID

The thyroid is a gland. You have glands throughout your body, where they create and release substances that help your body do a specific thing. Your thyroid makes hormones that help control many vital functions of your body. When your thyroid doesn't work properly, it can impact your entire body.

What to eat...

Salted nuts-

Brazil nuts, hazelnuts
are excellent sources
of selenium, which helps
support healthy thyroid
function.

Fresh eggs

Eggs contain healthy
amounts of both
selenium and iodine.
Eat the whole egg,
as the yolk holds
most of the nutrients.

Legumes and Beans

Beans and legumes
are rich, not
just in Zinc but
fibre as well.

Sunflower Seeds-

The sunflower seeds
are also loaded
with selenium.

Apples-

Apples are also
loaded with iodine.
The saying that
‘An Apple a day keeps
the doctor away’
is spot on!

What not to eat...

Foods With Soy
including Tofu

They have a potential
negative effect that
certain compounds in soy,
called isoflavones,
may have on the thyroid.

Cruciferous Vegetables
Like Broccoli-

Cruciferous vegetables,
such as broccoli may
interfere with the
production of thyroid
hormone if you have an
iodine deficiency

Gluten, Found in Bread-

Those with hypothyroidism
may want to consider
minimizing their intake of
gluten.

Fatty Foods Such as Butter,
Meat, and
All Things Fried-

Fats have been found
to disrupt the body's
ability to absorb thyroid
hormone replacement medicines

Alcohol-

Alcohol consumption can wreak
havoc on both thyroid
hormone levels in the
body and the ability of
the thyroid to
produce hormone
